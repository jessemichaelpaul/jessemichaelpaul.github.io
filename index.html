About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computational Mathematics at the University of North Carolina at Greensboro, expecting to graduate in Summer 2026. My research focuses on developing novel numerical methods for approximating viscosity solutions to fully nonlinear elliptic partial differential equations.

My dissertation focuses on developing a narrow stencil vanishing moment method for approximating viscosity solutions to the fully nonlinear Monge-AmpÃ¨re equation, achieving second-order accuracy with a novel 17-point finite difference scheme.

In addition to my dissertation, I have two ongoing research projects in representation theory: a probability-one homotopy method using the trivial projector onto E^G (implemented in Julia), and an Isotypic Finite Element Method (IFEM) with symmetry-aware assembly (implemented in MATLAB). These projects share my focus on fully nonlinear PDEs but are separate from my thesis work.

Beyond my primary research, I maintain interdisciplinary interests, including work in microhistory examining the intellectual origins of Plato's Philosopher Kings through the lens of the Pythagorean mathematician Archytas.

Research Interests

Numerical PDEs

Developing finite difference and finite element methods for fully nonlinear elliptic PDEs, with emphasis on the Monge-AmpÃ¨re equation and Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equations.

Probability-One Homotopy Method

Novel approach using the trivial projector onto E^G (group-invariant space) to compute viscosity (Alexandrov) solutions to fully nonlinear Monge-AmpÃ¨re equations. Implemented in Julia using HomotopyContinuation.jl.

Isotypic Finite Elements (IFEM)

Symmetry-aware assembly process using intertwining maps to preserve Schur's lemma up to the global system matrix. Achieves block diagonalization of global Jacobians. MATLAB implementation.

Applications

Optimal mass transport, antenna and reflector design, prescribed Gaussian curvature problems, and geostrophic fluid dynamics.

Dissertation Research

Narrow Stencil Finite Difference Methods for the Monge-AmpÃ¨re Equation

Core Contribution: Development of a novel 17-point narrow stencil scheme for the fully nonlinear Monge-AmpÃ¨re equation. Building on work by Feng and Lewis, I prove admissibility, stability, and convergence using a direct realization of the vanishing moment method.

Technical Innovation: The vanishing moment method allows for second-order accuracy while maintaining the narrow stencil property, crucial for computational efficiency and boundary condition handling.

Applications: Optimal mass transport, antenna and reflector design, prescribed Gaussian curvature problems, and geostrophic fluid dynamics.

Additional Research Projects

Beyond my dissertation, I have two ongoing papers applying representation theory to fully nonlinear PDEs:

1. Probability-One Homotopy (Julia): Implementation of a novel homotopy continuation method using the trivial projector onto the group-invariant space E^G. This approach computes viscosity (Alexandrov) solutions to the fully nonlinear Monge-AmpÃ¨re equation using HomotopyContinuation.jl.

2. Isotypic Finite Element Method (MATLAB): Development of symmetry-aware assembly algorithms using intertwining maps. This method preserves Schur's lemma structure up to the global system matrix, achieving block diagonalization of global Jacobians for efficient computation.
